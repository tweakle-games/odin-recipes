Pizza, Dough to Dish

Ingredients

For The Dough

  • Pizza Flour
  • Dried Yeast
  • Honey
  • Sea Salt
  • Water

For The Toppings

  • Tin Chopped Tomatos
  • Parsley
  • Basil
  • Cheddar Cheese
  • Mozzerella Cheese
  • Toppings Of Choice

Method

Starter

To begin the process, we need make a starter 24 hours in adavnce.

  1. Start with 300ml of water in a larger tupperware tub. Mix in 5g of dried yeast. Once mixed, add 5g of honey and mix again. Finally add 300g of pizza flour and mix until everything is combined.
  2. Place lig on tubberware and leave at room temperature for 1 hours. Then place in the fridge for 24 hours to allow the yeast to ferment.

Dough

Now your yeast has had time to ferment and grow up a bit. Its time to make the dough.

  1. Add 700ml of water to a large mixing bowl and mix the starter in until the start has melted in the water.
  2. Once combined, add 675g of flour to the mixture and mix until all the flour is coated.
  3. At this point add 40g of sea salt and mix thoroughly.
  4. Finally, add another 675g of flour and mix until everthing is combined.
  5. Now empty the mixture onto a clean surface top and begin kneading the dough. Simply push the dough in the centre with one hand and fold the dough back over its self and push again. Repeat this action for around 5 minutes and the dough should form into a stick glutton ball.
  6. Once you are happy with the consistency of the dough. We need to leave it covered on our surface for 20 minutes. This will take it from a sticky texture to a more smooth, workable state.
  7. Now our dough is in a smooth workable state, we need to oil up our hands with olive oil and smooth a little over the top of the dough. Run your hands along the edge and lift it from the surface. As we lift the dough it will begin to sag and stretch. This is what we want to happen as we begin to create a spine for the dough. We want to fold the stretched edges under our dough. Its very important we keep the top of the dough at the top as this becomes the spine and air can be trapped inside. Resulting in nice fluffy bases for our pizza.
  8. After around 10 folds we should have a nice smooth top on our dough. Add some oil to the mixing bowl we used earlier, coating the whole surface. Keeping our spine at the top we want to drop our ball of dough into the mixing bowl and leave covered for 2 hours to proove. In colder spaces you probably want to leave for longer. Italy is warm, 2 hours is fine there. UK is cold so I usually leave for 3-4 hours, especially in the winter.
  9. Now our dough have prooved up a bit. Its time to cut it into balls and get them ready for cooking or storing. If you dont have a dough cutter... get one! it will make this bit so much easier!!!
  10. I like a a lot of pizza so I usually cut my dough balls into 280-320g. Divide your dough into portions.
  11. With each portion you want to flip it over so the spine is on the work top and then pull the edges into the centre and work in a sort of star shape. This will trap air in the dough and help with the fluffyness. Next, flip it back over (spine side up). Cup it with your hands and gently drag it towards you to seal the folds in. Rotate the ball and do this 3-4 times to seal all around the base.
  12. Do the previous step with every portion you've cut out and then you are ready to move on.

    13. Storage

  13. To store the dough balls for a later occasion. Coat the dough balls in a thin layer of olive oil. Take some sandwhich bags (not zip tie ones you lunatic) and put your hand inside to turn the bag into a glove. Grab the ball and turn the bag inside out around it keeping the spine at the bottom of the bag. This way when you turn the bag inside out again to get the ball out. Your spine will still be at the top.
  14. Store in the freezer until you inevitably eat them all and have to make more dough.
